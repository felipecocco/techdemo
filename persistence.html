How do websites remember who you are?

After you open up your social media, or school email, or Google classroom site on your computer, you often don't have to log in again even if you close the window or open it. Or shut down your computer. Or go home and access it there. How is that possible?

You know computers have IP addresses, but those are determined by your network, not your device. At school, your computer is given a different address (and likely a different one every time you move around the school) than at home. How do your providers keep track of you as you move around?
